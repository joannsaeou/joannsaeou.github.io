The Danger of Change Approval Processes in Github

There is another term that the phrase "Change Approval Processes goes by: And that is called "Approving a Pull Request".

In Github, there is really a danger if changing the approval of the processes, because at first, there is the accident that happen due to a change control failure.

So if the team cannot prevent these risk from happening, they will have to take a step backs to enable the faster detection and recovery mode.

The second part to this accident is that there was a failure in testing. These failure of testing could be identified as cancelling the risky deployment.

To begin a pull request in Github, it must have a specific number of approving reviews from people with write or administration permissions in the repository before they can be merge, if there was few missing numbers in these then the merge could turn into a problem.

In github, if you can find a pull request where you or a team that you are a part of is requested for review with a specific qualifier. Then, there won't be any risk or danger involving this request.

While you are working with your team, with a low-trust, and following command-control culture group, the outcome of these changes could lead you into a worse results you may not like to see.

There are some tips I would suggest to avoid these type of issue that you or your team members are working on a project in a future and decided to deploy it.

  • Tip #1 - Repository owners and adminstrators in the team can merge a pull request even if it hasn't received an approving review, or if a reviewer who requested changes has left the team or is unavailable.
  • Tip # 2 - When several open pull requests each have a head branch pointing to the same commit, you will not be able to merge them if one or both have a pending or rejected reviews.
  • Tip # 3 - If a pull request you approved has changed significantly, you can dismiss your review. That way there won't be any confusion if your team or another person decided to leave the team and the reviews process will stay intact until the owners or admins request the Change approval

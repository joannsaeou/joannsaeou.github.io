What service-oriented architectures are?

SOA is a type of software design where services are provided to the other parts by application componnts thru a communication protocol over a network.

SOA is an operation that can be call on cautiously.

Enterprise Service Bus (ESB)

It is a middleware tool used to assign work among connected components of an API. Enterprise Service Bus are created to provide a uniform means of offering applications the ability to connect the bus and support to messages based on simple business policy rules.

Relationship between ESB vs SOA
- Both of ESB and SOA clean up junks caused by having lots of servers. SOA can make services available on an ESP in an SOA. .
- SOA was to design and built enterprise architecture around services(SOA) rather than complete applications..
- An ESB allows companies to adopt SOA principles without needing to rip and replace their entire framework. ESB gives compaies the flexibility to integrate a wide range of system, services and applications.
Advantages of SOA
- The main goal of SOA is to align business users with information technologies. Services enables increases business agility, enhanced reuseable, and a longer life space of API.
- Soa are easily maintained because it is elimination of the problems related with interacting services within an environment.
Disadvantages of a SOA architecture.

Extra overload- This is one of the disadvantages that SOA has because if you are double-dealing muliple services then it'll overload your system with further computation

High cost in SOA is disadvantages here because the expensive in terms of human resource, development time, and technology
